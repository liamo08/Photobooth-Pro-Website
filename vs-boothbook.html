Decision Guide

Which Do You
Actually Need?

You need BoothLedger if…

You're running events and need software that actually controls the booth: camera, prints, sharing, AI. Without this, there's no booth.

You need BoothBook (or a CRM) if…

You're getting enough enquiries that managing them by spreadsheet is breaking. Quotes, contracts, deposits, calendar — all the admin around bookings.

You probably need both if…

You're a full-time operator booking 30+ events a year. BoothLedger powers the experience; BoothBook (or similar) handles everything around it.

Alternatives to BoothBook

If you're shopping CRMs, Check Cherry, HoneyBook, Studio Ninja, and Dubsado are common alternatives. BoothLedger works alongside any of them.

FAQ

BoothLedger vs BoothBook
Common Questions

Is BoothBook the same as BoothLedger?

No. BoothBook is booking and CRM software. BoothLedger is the software that runs your photo booth at the event. Different jobs, different stages.

Do I need BoothBook if I have BoothLedger?

Only if you need bookings, contracts, and invoicing automation. BoothLedger doesn't do those. It's focused on running the booth itself.

Can I use BoothLedger and BoothBook together?

Yes — they don't conflict. BoothBook (or any CRM) handles your bookings; BoothLedger handles the booth.

Does BoothLedger handle bookings or invoicing?

Not currently. We're focused on being the best booth software. Bookings/invoicing are handled by tools like BoothBook, Check Cherry, HoneyBook, or Studio Ninja.
